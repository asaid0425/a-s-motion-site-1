spatial / design systems.

Defining when and where spatial metaphors actually help — and where depth, motion, and parallax add more cognitive load than they remove.

role · ux motion + systems scope · cross-surface years · 2019—2021
/ 01

the premise.

Every interface is a small world the user has to learn — where things live, what's still true after a tap, where a screen came from. Screens forget that history; users don't. A spatial system is the agreement that motion, depth, and position mean the same thing everywhere in the product.

space is not decoration. it's a property of use.

spatial topology · canonical map of an interface across time
/ 02

how the brain reads interface.

Three principles from cognitive science do almost all the work. Get these right and the rest of the system follows.

continuityp · 01
Users should understand where views come from and where they go. A screen that arrives without a path is one the brain has to place from scratch.
mental mapsp · 02
People form subconscious spatial maps of an app. The map exists whether or not the design serves it — designing for it pays compound interest.
cognitive loadp · 03
Abrupt change creates disorientation. Motion isn't decoration; it's how you tell the user what's still true after something moved.
card · seed → expansion → detail
subconscious spatial map · workspace
chrome held · centre transforms · details settle
/ 03

where it earns its keep.

Three patterns where spatial logic does the most work — deliberately boring on paper, because reliability is the point.

wayfindingu · 04
Users always know where they are, where they came from, and where they can go next. Anything less is a maze.
transformationu · 05
A control that becomes a surface teaches the rule once and forever — objects transforming between states create comprehension.
hierarchy & depthu · 06
The z-axis is the cheapest way to say "look here, but that's still there." Depth communicates importance and context.
three answers · where i am · came from · can go
action · composer · one object · two states
z-axis · foreground · background · modal
/ 04

motion with a job to do.

Motion is the visible part of the spatial system. Two jobs matter most:

latency maskingm · 07
Motion preserves perceived responsiveness. The system rarely gets faster after launch — what improves is what the user feels while it works.
gesture confirmationm · 08
Spatial response validates interaction. The interface saying "yes" is a small ritual — and the ritual is what makes the system feel alive.
action → skeleton → content · perceived vs measured
finger → object · response rides the hand
/ 05

one product. every surface.

Cross-surface work is where the system stops being a luxury. The same product runs on a phone in the field, a foldable on the desk, a headset on your face — and AI-native surfaces, where a conversation needs a geography to hold the work it produces. The spatial grammar is what keeps all of them feeling like one product.

close / the takeaway

design the grammar / not just the screen.

When a product has to live across surfaces, the spatial system is the part that survives the redesign. Get the grammar right and every team building on it stays coherent — without a meeting.
